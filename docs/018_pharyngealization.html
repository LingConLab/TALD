General chapter: Phonology

Pharyngealization is a complex feature that is realized as a constriction in the pharynx or epiglottis zone. Different scholars use different strategies for describing the locus of the pharyngealization: sometimes it is analyzed as a feature of the vowel (Forker 2013), or as a consonant feature (Khalilova 2009), or it is treated as a suprasegmental feature (Kibrik 1994; Moroz 2019). Pharyngealization commonly spreads through the word, although most researchers distinguish a core pharyngealized syllable (Kibrik 1994; Talibov 2007; Moroz 2019; Beljaev 2021). Some East Caucasian languages are known to have developed two different types of pharyngealization (e.g. Beljaev 2021), further complicating the picture. As we can see from the map, pharyngealization is common in central and southern Dagestan among languages of the Dargwa, Lak, Tsezic and Lezgic branches.
